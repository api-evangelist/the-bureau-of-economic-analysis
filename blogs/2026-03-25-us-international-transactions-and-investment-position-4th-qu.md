The U.S. current-account deficit resulting from international economic transactions narrowed by $48.4 billion, or 20.2 percent, to $190.7 billion in the fourth quarter of 2025, according to statistics released today by the U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis. The revised third-quarter deficit was $239.1 billion. The fourth-quarter deficit was 2.4 percent of current-dollar gross domestic product, down from 3.1 percent in the third quarter.The U.S. net international investment position, the difference between U.S. residents' foreign financial assets and liabilities, was -$27.54 trillion at the end of the fourth quarter of 2025. Assets totaled $42.96 trillion, and liabilities totaled $70.49 trillion.
